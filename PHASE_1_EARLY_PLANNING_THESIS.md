# Phase 1 Thesis Draft: Energy-Efficient Traffic Management

## 1. Proposed Study Title
Energy-Efficient and Cost-Aware Route Optimization for Time-Efficient Urban Traffic Management

## 2. Phase 1 Focus
This document covers only the early planning stage of the thesis. The scope is limited to the core research direction and does not include full experimentation, advanced modeling, or final conclusions.

Main focus areas:
- Energy efficiency in route planning
- Cost reduction in transport operations
- Reduction of energy cost and overall energy consumption
- Time efficiency through better route decisions

## 3. Background and Rationale
Urban traffic congestion increases fuel use, electricity use (for EV fleets), delivery delays, and operating expenses. In daily transport operations, inefficient routes cause longer travel time, unnecessary idling, and repeated stop-and-go movement. These conditions increase both energy consumption and travel cost.

An energy-efficient traffic management approach can help identify practical routes that balance:
- Lower energy use
- Lower operating cost
- Acceptable or improved travel time

This thesis starts by planning a framework that combines energy, cost, and time as joint decision criteria.

## 4. Problem Statement
Current route choices are often based only on distance or estimated travel time. This can lead to higher energy usage and higher trip cost, especially during congestion. There is a need for a route planning strategy that explicitly considers:
- Energy cost
- Total energy consumption
- Time efficiency

## 5. General Objective
To design an early-stage framework for traffic route planning that improves energy efficiency while reducing cost and maintaining time-efficient travel.

## 6. Specific Objectives (Phase 1)
1. Define measurable indicators for energy, cost, and time performance.
2. Identify relevant factors that influence energy consumption in traffic conditions.
3. Establish an initial method to compare candidate routes using energy-cost-time criteria.
4. Prepare baseline assumptions for simulation or prototype testing in later phases.

## 7. Initial Research Questions
1. How can route planning reduce energy consumption without causing major time delays?
2. What is the relationship between congestion level and energy cost per trip?
3. Which route decision factors provide the best balance between cost reduction and time efficiency?
4. How can an energy-aware route recommendation support practical transport operations?

## 9. Topics Covered in Initial Literature Search
The early search stays within the thesis topic and focuses on:

- Cost-aware vehicle routing and transport economics
- Fuel and electricity consumption models for road transport
- Congestion effects on idling, stop-and-go, and energy waste

## 10. Why This Study Is Important

### A. Cost Reduction
- Lower fuel or charging demand reduces daily operational spending.
- Better routes reduce idle time and unnecessary detours.
- Lower travel inefficiency can reduce maintenance pressure over time.

### B. Energy Cost and Consumption
- Energy-aware routing directly targets avoidable energy loss.
- Identifying high-consumption traffic patterns helps avoid expensive route choices.
- Reduced consumption supports sustainable transport operations.

### C. Time Efficiency
- Route decisions can avoid severe congestion windows.
- Better route balance can reduce late arrivals and schedule disruptions.
- Time-efficient movement improves service quality and planning reliability.

## 11. Initial Conceptual Framework (Input-Process-Output)

Input:
- Road network and route options
- Traffic condition indicators
- Energy price assumptions
- Trip demand or travel request

Process:
- Estimate energy consumption per route
- Estimate route cost per trip
- Estimate route travel time
- Compare and rank routes with a combined energy-cost-time score

Output:
- Recommended route with better efficiency balance
- Comparative report: baseline vs optimized option

## 12. Draft Indicators for Phase 1
- Estimated Energy Consumption per Trip (kWh or liters)
- Estimated Energy Cost per Trip
- Estimated Total Trip Cost
- Estimated Travel Time
- Time Efficiency Ratio

Simple planning formulas for later validation:
- Energy Cost per Trip = Estimated Energy Consumption x Unit Energy Price
- Time Efficiency Ratio = Baseline Travel Time / Optimized Travel Time
